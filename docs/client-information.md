# Client Information: Go Lease It

## Business Snapshot
- Company: Go Lease It
- Service: Commercial equipment lease financing
- Market: Canada (excluding Quebec)
- Existing tagline: "When your business needs it, Go Lease it."

## Website Goals
1. Present the brand as industry-agnostic, not only construction/trucking.
2. Drive qualified leads with clear calls to action and low-friction forms.
3. Support expansion into dental/professional and municipal segments.
4. Build trust through concise proof: process clarity, success stories, testimonials, and accessibility.

## Target Audience
- Small and mid-sized businesses needing equipment financing.
- Priority sectors:
  - Construction & Agriculture
  - Transportation & Logistics
  - Manufacturing & Mining
  - Dental & Professional Services
  - City and Municipal Public Works

## Client-Reported Pain Points
- Prospects often had poor prior broker experiences.
- Prospects often do not understand commercial lease financing.

## Positioning and Voice
- Straightforward, practical, and easy to reach.
- Emphasize direct contact and availability (including evenings/weekends).
- Use concise, plain-language copy.
- Avoid long, generic AI-style paragraphs.

## Information Architecture Baseline
Top-level navigation:
1. Home
2. Financing Solutions
3. Industries We Serve
4. How It Works
5. About Go Lease It
6. Contact

Expected section structure:
- Financing Solutions: Equipment Leasing, Sale-Leaseback, Auction & Private Sales, Rental Conversions & Repairs
- Industries We Serve: five sector groups above
- How It Works: Leasing Process, Leasing vs Buying, FAQ
- About: Our Story & Values, Go Lease It Advantage, Success Stories

## Conversion Requirements
- Primary CTA language:
  - `Apply Now`
  - `Get in Touch` / `Get a Free Quote`
- Keep top-funnel forms short (roughly 3 essential fields) before full application.
- Keep phone/contact access visible in the header, with mobile click-to-call.
- Multi-step application flow is desired; e-signature integration is a future-state option.

## Content Requirements
- Success stories should be industry-categorized and narrative-driven (who, where, need, outcome).
- Testimonials should be curated from real customer reviews.
- Include a simple visual leasing process summary (5-step style preferred).
- Keep sections scannable: short headings, bullets, icons, concise blocks.

## Design Direction
- Keep existing Go Lease It brand assets unless updated by client.
- Palette direction: blue and green.
- Tone: clean, modern, professional.
- Use industry-diverse imagery/icons; avoid overemphasis on heavy construction.

## Competitive Context
- Known competitor references include RB Auction financial services and similar leasing firms.
- Differentiator to emphasize: responsiveness, accessibility, and clear communication.
